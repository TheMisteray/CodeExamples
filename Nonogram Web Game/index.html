Welcome to Picross! The nonogram puzzle game.

Here's what you need to know to play:

The grid contains many tiles, some are marked tiles, and some are blank (In figure, marked tiles are blue).

Your goal is to find all of the marked tiles.

Your only hints as to where these marked tiles are are the numbers on the top and left of the grid (As shown below).

The numbers indicate groups of marked tiles on the respective row or column. For example having the number 6 in this column means that there is a group of 6 connected marked tiles in this column.

Multiple numbers mean multiple groups, so -2 1 3- means that there are a group of 2, a single tile, and a group of 3 active tiles this row/column.

What is also important is that the groups MUST ALSO appear in that order (from left to right for rows and top to bottom for columns).

For example, in the case of this hint to the right:

There is a group of 4 active tiles, followed by a group of 3 active tiles, then a single active tile.

You must use these hints to uncover all of the marked tiles to complete the puzzle.

To check a tile as marked, click on the tile. You may also mark a tile as empty by right clicking.

Additionally you can click and drag to mark all tiles you touch quickly (This can be disabled in settings).

If you incorrectly mark a tile, the correct tile will be shown with an X over it. This is a miss.

Your number of misses will be tracked on the right. Get as few as you can!

Lastly, this is the hint button:

Clicking the hint button, and then clicking on a tile will reveal that tile correctly every time! (Game area will change color while in hint mode).

However, you only get one hint.

Make sure to ust it wisely, sometimes the puzzle can have two equally likely solutions, and this hint may be your only way to figure out which is correct!

That's everything, have fun!
